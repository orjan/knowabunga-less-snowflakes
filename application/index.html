##LeetHack Alpha 2

So it's time again! After the first glorious late-night LeetHack we are back in business once more! For those of you that missed out on the initial alpha in Malmö at last knowabunga, Leethack was a reaction to another event which wasn't very good, produced by someone else. It's basically a gamified hackathon where you, in the case of Leethack, conquer nodes by programmatically solving puzzles. The first event we held was very much an attempt to just make sure we could produce something that worked. It wasn't flawless and I think we ended up starting a couple of minutes or hours after the specified time but it worked. And there were bugs.
We've solved a lot of bugs (no more clone-wars!), added more challenges/nodes and done a whole lot of improvements pretty much everywhere.
We also have better prizes this time! The prizes last time were of course fabulous, if you were into strange German films and weird bubblegum machines, but now there will be fancy (somewhat) programmable hardware, some stickers and candy!

The contest will be run in pairs that we'll create. We would prefer it if you sign up in the Dojo which will make it easier for us to create teams beforehand, but if you don't we'll fix it up before we start (as long as you show up and all). You can use any programming language you want (including Elm) and, unlike other similar events, any way you solve the puzzles are valid as long as we get a proper answer.

There will be quite a lot of prizes so join up!

##Hardware geek out! Let’s build your first wifi enabled hardware device!

Computers are awesome but when it comes to working with sensors they are not best choice, luckily for us micro controllers are not only easy to program they are also super cheap. In this workshop you will learn how to build a wifi enabled temperature sensor with NodeMCU, DS18B20 and connect it to a local service or the azure IoT service bus.

##Technology Future Map

It would be really interesting to do a technology future map together! This is something we should do and keep maintaining it every Knowabunga event. It would help us focusing on the right stuff (on a high level of course), and help us take a strategic direction for what to put our efforts in.

Maintaining it will also lead to more awereness in former insights and not just accepting that things didn’t go as we thought it would - it probably didn’t happen at all or it happened way faster then anticipated.

In time we will also see what types of things that catalysed others. In terms of forecasting this is really important to understand.

Peter Silverud explains the concept here: https://youtu.be/M05g0JRcUw0?t=21m53s
